INTERNATIONAL PREPAID CARDS

A list of debit cards that you can order home and use everywhere
  • Anyone: You can have your own prepaid card without ever being a bank customer. You order it online, receive it by mail, receive money anywhere in the world and spend it wherever cards are accepted. No background checks needed, just a few scommon documents.
  • Anywhere: The card arrives in your mailbox, in a simple envelope, usually within one or two weeks from ordering. It can only be used upon confirmation and activation, so in case anyone else picks it up there is no risk of money loss.
  • Anytime: The balance and operations can always be checked online, upon authentication. Online banking is always included.
  1. ID: Scan or picture of your National ID or passport
  2. Proof of residence: An utility bill, no older than 3 months, with your name and address on it.
  3. Contact details: A functional e-mail address that will be used for authentication.
  4. 2nd factor authentication: Active mobile phone number able to receive SMS messages
  5. Payment instrument: A card or account that allows you to make a first payment if needed. Upfront payment is not always mandatory, but usually those who ask for it will offer cheaper services later.
  6. Other documents: Some service providers may ask for additional documents in order to raise limits, including notarized documents or contracts/bills for processing large sums.
  • ATM fees: ATM costs are quite high - with fixed fees favoring high withdrawals and percentage fees favoring low withdrawals. Failed withdrawals are usually charged as well.
  • POS fees: Usually payment in stores or online should be free, but be cautios that this is not the case with all service providers
  • Card funding: Some platforms will charge a fee even for transfering money between the e-wallet and the card
  • Banking transfer: Cheap or free transfers are with SEPA accounts, while SWIFT transfers are usually more expensive
  • Currency exchange: Whenever the payment currency is different from the card currency there may be a 2-3% conversion charge. Some cards will use the better exchange rates from Mastercard or Visa.
  • Inactibity fee: Some accounts may charge higher fees after a while of inactivity. Cautiously read the full list of fees before ordering!
  • Savings: Most of the times there is no public deposit guarantee while issuers are harder to trace, so the cards are not the best for medium and long term savings.
  • Chargeback: Payments are not as easy to cancel, for extra shopping protection credit cards are always better.
  • Foreign currency: If most payments involve currency exchange, locally denominated cards may be the better option
  • Online commerce: Some platforms (usually their "business" versions) will allow receiving payments from customers.
  • Mass payments: Most platforms will allow for transfers to multiple recipients at once.
  • IBAN accountt: Most issuers will use sub-accounts, but there are a few who assign each user with their own individual IBAN.
  • Virtual cars: If your in a hurry, there may be the option of asking for a virtual card, issued instantly and usually cheaper than its plastic counterpart - you get a card number, an expiry date and the CVC - that you can use for online payments.
  • Anonymousity: Some issuers will take the application without asking for documents, in which case there will be limits on the total payments. Limits are lifted as soon as documents are submitted. These "anonymous" card can be also used if you want to order the card for someone else - as a gift or for a child.
  • Bitcoin Transfer: Some cards can be anonymously and cheaply funded via Bitcoin. Beware of price volatitlity and technical procedure, or Bitcoin can forever be lost.
  1. Buy online without exposing your main accounts.
  2. Travel without worrying of card loss or theft.
  3. Transfer money with lower fees.
  4. Invest in forex, gold or bitcoin while keeping easy access to the money.
  5. Gamble online from anywhere, without being blacklisted.
  6. Keep your credit rating high by avoiding unnecessary contact with banks
  7. Protect your financial privacy against people who may have too easy access to the data.
  8. Shelter your income from garnishment.
  9. Avoid the blocking of the banking system, as experienced by the people of Greece or Cyprus.
  10. Keep an emergency stash, in case you need to leave your home in a hurry.
